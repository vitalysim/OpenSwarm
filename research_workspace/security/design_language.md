# Security Research Design Language

Use a clear, evidence-first visual system:

- Prefer neutral backgrounds, high-contrast text, and restrained accent colors.
- Use severity color only for risk semantics: critical red, high orange, medium amber, low blue, informational gray.
- Label timelines, exploit chains, and diagrams with exact dates, source names, and confidence.
- Avoid sensational imagery. Visuals should help a reader verify claims and understand impact.
- For public writing, separate confirmed facts, analyst assessment, and open questions.
